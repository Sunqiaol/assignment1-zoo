Penguin

Penguins are flightless water birds known for their distinctive black and white plumage. These fascinating creatures are well-suited to life in the cold, harsh environments of the Southern Hemisphere, found mainly in Antarctica, South Africa, Australia, New Zealand and South America. Penguins are incredibly social animals, living in large colonies, with some populations consisting of hundreds of thousands of individuals. They are also known for their distinctive waddling gait and ability to swim at high speeds underwater, with some species capable of reaching depths of over 500 feet.
