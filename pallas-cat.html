Pallas's Cat

The Pallas cat, also known as the Manuel cat, is a small wild cat native to the grasslands and mountain steppes of Central Asia. It is named after German zoologist Peter Simon Pallas, who first described the species in the late 18th century. Pallas cats have a distinctive body shape, with round heads, short legs, and dense fur adapted to their cold, arid habitat. It is a solitary and elusive animal that feeds on small mammals and birds and can survive in harsh environments that few other animals can.
